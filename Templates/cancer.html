Steps to Claim Cancer Insurance

One critical factor in choosing health insurance with cancer cover is the easy claim procedure and settlement.

The following **Steps need to be followed while requesting an insurance claim.

Step 1: Claim Notification

  • First, a claim or request should be submitted within 7 days of the potential loss or when the loss begins. In any event, this should be completed on time for at most 30 days.

Step 2: Submit the Claim Form or Evidence

  • The claim form and the documents required for evidence should be submitted within 30 days of the loss.

  • Even so, if you can provide a valid reason for not submitting the documents on time, your claim might not be rejected.

  • Usually, this is acceptable if the circumstances are not under the insured's control, such as a medical emergency.

Step 3: Supporting Documents and Examination

  • All documents and medical records related to your treatment should be submitted within 15 days of your discharge from the hospital.

  • Failing to submit them might be acceptable under exceptional circumstances.

Step 4: Claim Settlement

  • If all the documents are valid and credible, we will provide you with the claim in a lump sum as soon as possible.

Documents Required for Filing a Cancer Insurance Claim

  • Medical Records

  • Cancer diagnosis proof

  • Complete history of cancer treatment, such as records of chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and other procedures.

  • Details of insurance policy and coverage

  • Proof of medical expenses such as bills and invoices.

  • Legal documents such as your identity proof, address proof, etc.
